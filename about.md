## Origin Story

In 2018, **Geoff Scott** started something simple: a free, monthly morning gathering in NYC called *Startup Lean Coffee*. The idea was borrowed from the [Lean Coffee™](https://leancoffee.org/) format — a lightly facilitated but agenda-less meeting where participants show up, propose topics, vote, and talk.

No keynote speakers. No pitch decks. Just founders, investors, operators, advisors, and executives sitting around tables having genuine, raw conversations about the things that actually keep them up at night.

The conversations have spanned fundraising, product-market fit, go-to-market strategy, AI, co-founder conflict, hiring, pivots, and everything in between. More importantly, the relationships formed around those tables have led to real outcomes — members have become co-founders, grown their teams with key hires, found valuable resources, and built lasting friendships.

In 2025, **Benjamin Friedman** and **Karina Muller** joined as co-organizers to grow and formalize our community, which now includes over 1,200 founders, advisors, and investors. It was clear that "Startup Lean Coffee" no longer captured what this had become. It wasn't just about startups. It wasn't just about coffee. It was about what happens when you pull up a chair, bring a real question, and trust the people around you to help you think it through.

So we gave it the name it deserved: **Table**.

Today, Table meets every month in NYC. We're adding new events and expanding to new cities. Our events bring together a growing network of builders, operators, and investors who believe that the best insights come from honest conversation — not polished presentations.

There's always a seat for you at Table.

## Aha moments from recent Tables

A sampling of ideas that came out of recent conversations:

- Using AI is a skill — not just a tool.
- Ensure proper legal setup of your corp early.
- Crunchbase for research: who's writing checks, who's receiving them.
- Fundraising is a full-time job.
- Using Claude with MCP.
- Niche selling — focus on smaller groups.
- Attending events to make contacts.
- Warm intros are the best fundraising channel.
- Be open to guidance, advice, and mentorship.
- Genuine storytelling.
- AI could be an opportunity.
- Offer value when trying to get someone's attention.
- Empathy over persuasion.
- Communication is key with your team.
- Strong advisors and mentors are key to a founder's success.
